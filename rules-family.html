PBR Housing Rules and Regulations - Family Housing

website copy published on January 22, 2023
Family Housing Family Units

A. Care of Houses and Surroundings

1. The occupants shall be responsible for the protection and care of the house assigned to them including its surrounding grounds and ornamental plants. Occupants are required to maintain the lawn and make it presentable.

2. Request for normal house repair must be accompanied by a Work Request form to be submitted to the General Services Supervisor for approval and action. Work involving modifications/additions of house structures must be coursed through the General Services Supervisor for approval by Administration Manager or higher management.

3. Cutting or major trimming of big trees shall be coordinated with the General Services Supervisor for evaluation & implementation schedule. Cutting of big trees requires permit from DENR, therefore, shall be with prior approval of Administration Manager.

4. Occupants must report the presence of destructive insects in the house, lawns and trees and request the spraying/treatment to the Pest Control Contractor who will regularly visit the premises.

B. Pets and Hunting

1. Pets should be kept within each individual house premises.

2. Dogs must be penned in or kepton leash.

3. Security may dispose off stray dogs if they are seen to be nuisance in the compound or threat to safety of others.

4. Pet cats should be tagged to prevent being caught and disposed off when it became nuisance to other residents.

5. Constructions of permanent enclosures to contain pets (cats, dogs, etc.) are subject to Management approval.

6. Hunting in any form is prohibited within the compound.

C. Residents’ Children and Maids

1. Residents’ should not allow minors (7 years and below) to play at the main thoroughfares without anybody attending to them. For the safety purposes, playing or biking at the main road from housing gate to Main House No. 16 is prohibited.

2. Children should be advised against entering vacant houses.

3. Children should be restrained from playing in the golf fairways, green and bunkers and at the Clubhouse.

4. Children below 14 years old are not allowed at the gym. The gym should be utilized as intended.

5. Residents shall register their house helpers and other household service employees with the Security Office.

D. Vehicle Movement and Compound Traffic

1. All residents’ should secure stickers from Security office for their vehicle. Residents and guest should follow traffic signs and regulations.

2. Maximum speed limit within the compound including TPS and BOQ is strictly 30 kph only.

3. Tricycles and motorcycles are not allowed inside the compound.

4. Practice driving is prohibited within the compound.

5. Vehicles are not allowed to traverse and park on grass areas (mini-park and golf fairways).

6. Vehicles window should be rolled down and cabin lights should be turned on (at night) upon entering and leaving the gate for proper identification.

E. Security, Safety and Conservation Measures

1. Residents should lock their house (doors, windows, etc.) before leaving the compound.

2. Residents may advise the Chief Security Officer whenever the house is left unoccupied overnight and during weekends.

3. All home appliances should be unplugged whenever occupants leave the house unattended.

4. Unattended watering of lawns and hosing of driveways is prohibited.

5. Residents should always practice electricity and water conservation measures. Water leaks shall be reported to the General Services Supervisor for immediate action.

6. Housing residents, guests and house helps should always use the Main Gate when entering or leaving the Housing Compound.

F. Residents’ House Guests and Visitors

1. The residents shall be responsible for the conduct of their guests within the compound.

2. Visitors will be allowed to enter the compound only upon the approval of the housing residents to be visited.

3. Housing resident’s visitor shall only be allowed entry through Housing Gate.

4. Visitors entering the compound shall deposit their firearms or any deadly weapon with the housing gate guard for safekeeping, except military personnel and peace officers.

5. Residency in the compound is strictly for the employee and his immediate family only. House guests or boarders staying on a permanent status are prohibited. Relatives and friends staying for more than two (2) weeks must first be cleared with the Administration Manager.

6. Activities that may disturb or bring inconvenience to the residents during rest periods shall be avoided in the compound.

7. Guest shall be accompanied by responsible member of the family when using housing sports and recreational facilities.

G. Vendors and Solicitors

1. Vendors and solicitors will be allowed to enter the compound only upon the approval of the housing residents to be visited. However, said vendors/solicitors should be advised by the approving residents to limit themselves only within his premises. Residents wanting to refer vendors/solicitors to other residents must first seek permission from the latter.

H. Ownership/Possession and Use of Firearm

1. Only short, low caliber and duly licensed firearms are allowed to be possessed and kept inside occupants’ residences. Other types and high caliber firearms should be deposited at the Refinery’s Security Office if not needed. All firearms must be registered with the Refinery Security office.

2. Practice firing inside the compound is strictly prohibited.

3. Firearms should be kept locked and out of reach by children to avoid accidental firing.

4. Explosives (grenades, dynamites, plastic explosives and explosive compounds) and pyrotechnic materials are prohibited from being brought into the compound.

I. Golf Course

1. The Golf Course is open to golf members, BOQ and housing residents and their houseguests, including official company guests. Membership fees and monthly dues are imposed on those electing to be regular members of the Peninsula Golf Club.

2. Membership and use of the Golf Course by other individuals or groups shall be subject to approval of the Peninsula Golf Club Officers (PGCO). Request for special tournaments shall be coordinated with PGCO and approved by the Vice President.

3. Sponsored house guests can be accommodated with prior arrangements (if golf course is available).

4. The Golf Course is closed every Friday for maintenance purposes.

J. Use of Sports, Wellness Facilities and other Amenities

1. The Peninsula Clubhouse is open for use by golfers, BOQ/Housing residents and their dependents/guest during its normal operating hours everyday from 7:00 am to 4:00 pm except Fridays.

2. Use of the facilities for other purposes shall be coordinated with the General Services Supervisor and shall be approved by the Administration Manager.
